Who Were the Signers

Famous Signers

Some of the most famous signers of the Declaration of Independence include:

Thomas Jefferson
Principal author of the Declaration; later became the 3rd U.S. President.
John Adams
Strong advocate for independence; later became the 2nd U.S. President.
Benjamin Franklin
Renowned inventor, diplomat, and statesman; helped secure French support during the Revolution.
John Hancock
President of the Second Continental Congress; known for his large and bold signature.
Samuels Adams
Major revolutionary figure and political leader in Massachusetts.
George Wythe
Influential legal scholar and mentor to several Founding Fathers.
Charles Carroll of Carrollton
Only Catholic signer and one of the wealthiest men in the colonies.
Roger Sherman
Only person to sign all four major U.S. founding documents.

These individuals all played a significant role in the founding of the United States and are remembered for their courage in signing this document, which was considered treasonous by the reigning country of Great Britain
