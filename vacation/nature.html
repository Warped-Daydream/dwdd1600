Wildlife, Nature, & Hiking

Animals

Okunoshima
Also known as 'Bunny Island', this island is covered in hundreds of fluffy, friendly rabbits that roam freely across its landscapes.
Aoshima
Similar to Okunoshima, but instead of bunnies, it's cats! This island is home to only a handful of people and over a hundred cats!
Zao Fox Village
In the moutains of Miyagi, the Zao Fox Village is, as the name suggests, full of foxes. Featuring six different breeds of fox, these half-wild creatures are quite friendly!

Gardens/Nature Sightseeing

Gardens of Kenrokuen

Ranked as one of the top three gardens in Japan, it features ponds, streams, old trees, teahouses, and, of course, various plants and flowers.

Miyajima Island

An island featuring a nature walk through a valley, various hiking trails (though has a cable car for those wanting an easier walk) and even a monkey park!

Hikes

Nakasendo Highway Hike

A walk along the Nakasendo Highway, approximately 7.7 kilometers/4.8 miles of scenic forests, farming villages, and old post towns.

Asahidake Trek

Mt Asahidake is covered in short, circular walks highlighting lakes, waterfalls, and alpine flowers in late july.
